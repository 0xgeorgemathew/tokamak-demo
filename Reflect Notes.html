Based on the CSS styles provided in the HTML file, the size of the .hero-black-hole container, Desktop (default): Width: 1440px Height: 810px The Key Technique: CSS Masking The container .hero-black-hole holds the video. Instead of having a border, it has a mask-image property applied to it. A mask works like a stencil: White areas of the mask let the content (the video) show through completely. Transparent areas of the mask hide the content. Gray or semi-transparent areas make the content partially visible, creating a fade effect. How the Mask Works The specific mask used is not an image file but a dynamically generated radial-gradient. Here is the relevant CSS rule from the file: Generated css .hero-black-hole { -webkit-mask-image: radial-gradient(50% 50% at 50% 50%, #fff 60.94%, transparent 100%); mask-image: radial-gradient(50% 50% at 50% 50%, #fff 60.94%, transparent 100%); } Use code with caution. Css Let's break down that radial-gradient: radial-gradient(...): It creates a circular gradient. 50% 50% at 50% 50%: This creates a perfect circle positioned exactly in the center of the container. #fff 60.94%: The gradient is solid white (#fff) from the center out to 60.94% of its radius. This is the area where the video is fully visible. transparent 100%: From 60.94% to 100% of the radius, the gradient fades from solid white to completely transparent. This creates the soft, feathered edge. In simple terms: A soft, circular stencil is placed over the rectangular video. This makes the center of the video visible and smoothly fades out the edges and corners to transparency, hiding the video's natural rectangular shape and making it look like it's part of the circular, swirling black hole graphic.
